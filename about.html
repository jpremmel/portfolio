About the programmer

Jacqueline Remmel

Welcome to my programming portfolio! On this site you will find links to projects I have completed. So far, many of them were created as part of the curriculum at Epicodus, where I am learning how to code.

My interest in programming began during my junior year of college. I attended Willamette University in Salem, Oregon, where I double-majored in Physics and French. As part of my Physics major, I was required to take a programming course. I enjoyed the class a lot and I was surprised to find that I was pretty good at coding, so I took a second computer science course. In hindsight, I may have switched to a computer science major had I taken those courses earlier! But it was too late to change majors at that point, so I made a mental note to explore programming more in the future after graduating.

For two years after college, I bounced around between career ideas: I contemplated grad school, medical school, and optometry school. I worked in optometry offices for a while before coming back to programming. I am originally from the California Bay Area, so I'd grown up immersed in the Silicon Valley tech scene. I'd worked a summer job at a startup company that had given me a taste of what working as a software developer could be like. Once I started researching coding schools, it became clear pretty quickly that Epicodus would likely be a great next step...so here I am, and I can't wait to see where this journey takes me next!

I currently live in Portland, Oregon with my partner and my dog, Sylvie. I enjoy road cycling, jigsaw puzzles, baking, singing, and reading. Aside from becoming an all-star programmer, I hope to one day be an excellent piano player as well. Thanks for visiting my website!
